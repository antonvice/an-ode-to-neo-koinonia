# Storage

Storage is the quiet half of autonomy.

Agents need memory: preferences, histories, contracts, identities, permissions, logs, training material, and evidence. Entertainment factories need worlds. Research systems need records. Institutions need audit trails. Without storage, intelligence becomes amnesiac and power becomes unaccountable.

The future struggle over data is not only privacy. It is custody. Who keeps the memory that agents use to act on behalf of a person? Who can move it? Who can erase it? Who can prove what happened?

Storage is where personal continuity and social accountability meet.
