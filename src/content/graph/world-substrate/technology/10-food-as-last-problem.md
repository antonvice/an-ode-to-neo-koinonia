# Food as Last Problem

Food may be one of the last ordinary goods to become fully automated.

Software can be generated. Furniture can be printed. Machines can be assembled by other machines. But food sits at the intersection of biology, safety, energy, taste, freshness, habit, culture, and trust.

Lab food machines may eventually grow proteins, nutrients, and meals close to the point of use. Automated farms may become more robotic and more controlled. But food is not only input. It is ritual, comfort, identity, and risk.

This makes food a hard frontier for autonomy. A civilization that can automate food has crossed from digital abundance into biological production.
