# Research and Verification

The future human economy may narrow around two activities: research and verification.

Research expands the boundary of the possible. Verification protects society from hallucination, fraud, error, and premature certainty. AI can generate enormous volumes of claims, designs, plans, simulations, media, and code. But generation is not truth. A civilization of infinite output needs a civilization-scale discipline of checking.

This makes verification a central social function. Humans may spend less time producing routine artifacts and more time asking whether artifacts are real, useful, safe, original, aligned, and worth integrating into the world.

Research asks: what else can be true?

Verification asks: is this true enough to act on?
