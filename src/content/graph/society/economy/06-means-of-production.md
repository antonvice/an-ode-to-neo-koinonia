# Means of Production

The core assets of the future are not apps. They are means of production.

A means of production is any machine or system that can produce useful output: compute clusters, storage systems, robot fleets, lab-food machines, 3D printers, factories for furniture and goods, machines that produce parts for other machines, and computers that orchestrate all of them.

As software becomes easier to generate, the scarce layer shifts downward into matter and energy. The question is no longer who can build the interface. The question is who owns the machine, who has access to compute, who controls the production process, and who can verify the output.

This creates a new access economy. Those who cannot afford the machines rent them, borrow them, subscribe to them, or participate in shared ownership networks. The economy becomes less about selling software and more about lending autonomy.
