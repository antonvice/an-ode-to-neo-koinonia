# AI Bubble

The AI bubble is not that AI is useless. The bubble is that many people mistake newly cheap software for durable value.

When software becomes easy to produce, more software appears. A person can turn a job they once performed into a small interface, attach an agent, add onboarding, and call it a company. The product may work. It may even earn money for a while. But temporary usefulness is not the same as deep value.

The deeper question is whether the company controls a frontier. Does it own scarce compute? Does it build machines? Does it produce knowledge? Does it verify reality? Does it coordinate agents with real systems? Or does it merely wrap a workflow that every user will soon hand to their own agent?

The bubble bursts when the market learns to separate capability from packaging.
