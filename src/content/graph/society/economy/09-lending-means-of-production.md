# Lending Means of Production

If autonomy is produced by machines, then inequality becomes a question of access to machines.

Some people will own compute, storage, robots, printers, food machines, and energy systems. Others will need to rent them. The old subscription economy sold access to software. The new access economy sells access to production itself.

This does not only mean factories. It also means local machines that perform domestic labor, personal devices that host AI models, shared robot fleets, community food systems, and workshops where physical goods can be generated from designs.

The political question is whether productive autonomy becomes a luxury or a commons. Neo-Koinonia must ask who gets to command machines, who only borrows them, and what forms of collective ownership can prevent autonomy from becoming another enclosure.
