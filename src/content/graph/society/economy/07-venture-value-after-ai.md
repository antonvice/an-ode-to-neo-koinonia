# Venture Value After AI

AI coding makes many startups look larger than they are.

A small team can now produce the appearance of a software company: polished UI, onboarding, dashboards, integrations, agents, internal tools. But if the product is merely a thin automation of a known business task, it may not deserve venture-scale value. It is not a deep company. It is a temporary wrapper around a capability that users may soon command directly.

The useful ventures are those that move the frontier: research, compute, robotics, hardware, production systems, scientific tools, verification systems, food automation, energy, and protocols that let agents coordinate with real-world systems.

Everything else risks becoming investor theater: software built because AI made it easy to build, not because it creates durable value.
