# Goods, Services, and Research

From first principles, economic life can be divided into three categories: goods, services, and research.

Goods are physical outputs: food, housing materials, tools, furniture, computers, machines. Services are performed transformations: booking, cleaning, repairing, accounting, transporting, organizing, producing, maintaining. Research is the expansion of what can be known and therefore what can later be automated, produced, or verified.

AI compresses services first. Any service that can be described, checked, and executed through software becomes a candidate for personal automation. Booking tickets, paying taxes, managing campaigns, filling forms, reconciling accounts, and planning logistics become tasks for one's own agent rather than external SaaS companies.

Physical services fall later, but not outside the same logic. Once robots become cheap and capable enough, physical service also becomes agentic labor. The agent does not merely plan the service. It sends a body to perform it.

In this world, the durable frontier is research: discovering what should be done, what is true, what is safe, what is valuable, and whether AI-generated outputs can be trusted.
